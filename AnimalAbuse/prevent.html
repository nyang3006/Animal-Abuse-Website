Prevent


This page is to show people signs of when an animal is
being abuse and how to stop it. We want the best for animals.
These facts will tell you tips on what to do when you see an animal being abused
and how to stop circuses from harming any more animals.


How to Report Animal Cruelty

Report to local animal control departments, animal shelters or humane society.
If you are traveling or in a community without an animal control, then call 911 to report animal abuse.


How to tell if a pet is being abused:

  • ~ Tight collar that causes the neck to have wounds and become embedded in the pets neck.


  • ~ Open wounds that cannot be treated


  • ~ Skin condition (ex: loss hair, bump or rash)


  • ~ Extreme thinness.


  • ~ Fur infested with ticks, fleas or other parasites.


  • ~ Signs of untreated grooming, such as overgrown claws and dirty coat.

Environmental signs of cruelty:

  • - Pets are tied up and left outside without water or food.


  • - Pets left in an area with dangerous objects that can harm them (ex: feces, garbage. broken glass etc)


  • - Animals housed in kennels or cages (sometimes with other animals) that are too small
    to allow them to stand, walk, turn around or make any movements.


  • - Kept inside a car during a hot day without water or windows opened.


How can you help stop circuses:

  • * Do not attent circuses that features wild animals or participate in wild animal "rides"


  • * Spread the word on how the animals are being treated in circuses


  • * Ban the use of bullhook or chains for elephants
